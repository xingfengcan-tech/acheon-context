# Official no-video contribution decision

This decision records the publication structure selected for Acheon. None of the
three active routes below requires a YouTube upload.

## Identity

- Legal submitter: the actual individual rights holder, using their truthful legal
  first and last name and a durable contact email.
- Public project author: `Acheon by xingfengcan-tech`.
- GitHub identity: `xingfengcan-tech`.
- Do not present `Acheon`, `xingfengcan-tech`, or `Acheon contributors` as a legal
  entity unless such an entity actually exists and the submitter is authorized to
  bind it.

The legal name and email are intentionally not stored in this repository. They
must be supplied by the rights holder directly in the official form.

## Copyright and repository license

- Retain project copyright; do not assign the project or private research to
  OpenAI.
- Public notice: `Copyright (c) 2026 Acheon contributors`.
- Public repository license: MIT.
- Submit only files in `disclosure-manifest.json`. Private prototype theories,
  derivations, prompts, credentials, conversations, and paid-call payloads remain
  excluded.

## Showcase

Use the actual individual rights holder as the agreement party and
`Acheon by xingfengcan-tech` as the public display author. The prepared GitHub URL
and public architecture image satisfy the project and cover-link fields; no video
field is required.

The rights holder must personally verify ownership or authorization for the code,
copy, and cover, then accept the Showcase Program Agreement. That agreement gives
OpenAI a non-exclusive, worldwide, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use, test,
store, copy, translate, display, modify, distribute, and promote the submitted
materials. It does not transfer Acheon's copyright, but the granted Showcase
license is irrevocable.

Official form: <https://openai.com/form/showcase-submission/>

## OpenAI Evals

Contribute only the four staged registry files for
`long-horizon-context-integrity`; do not contribute the Acheon implementation or
private derivation. The contribution uses the upstream MIT license.

Before a pull request:

1. an independent human reviews all 24 tasks, criteria, reference answers, all 16
   candidates, and every provisional label;
2. the files pass LF/hash, Git LFS, registry, dummy-run, and supported-model checks
   in the current upstream checkout; and
3. the rights holder personally confirms adequate data rights, MIT licensing,
   Usage Policies compliance, future service-improvement use, and limited
   availability of review or benefits.

Official repository: <https://github.com/openai/evals>

## Developer Community

Publish under the `xingfengcan-tech` public identity in the Community category,
using the editor's current project tag. Link the MIT repository and use only the
reviewed public copy. Forum login, authentication challenges, final review, and
topic creation remain account-holder actions.

Official entry point: <https://developers.openai.com/community>

## Human-only final gates

Automation may prepare, validate, and prefill project facts. It must not invent a
legal identity, assert unverified rights, complete item-level human review, accept
an agreement, solve authentication challenges, or click a final legal submission
on the rights holder's behalf.
